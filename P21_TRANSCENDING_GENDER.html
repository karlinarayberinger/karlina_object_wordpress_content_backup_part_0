The purpose of this web page is to succinctly introduce the topic of sexual dimorphism and a modern way to think about gender as a cultural construct (especially with respect to humans and the transhuman cyborgs they seem destined to become within the next 100 years (if humans are able to survive that long and if their technology (especially artificial intelligence) continues to evolve at the exponential rate it has).

PART_1: Origins of Sexual Dimorphism (on Planet Earth)

According to the cumulative body of scientific knowledge which human scientists (and their “tools” (especially computers)) have generated thus far, the first organisms on Planet Earth were single-celled organisms which reproduced asexually by dividing into two separate organisms.

With each successive generation of such organisms, the culmination of “random” genetic copying errors resulted in a mutation (i.e. a digital copying error which results from relatively chaotic interference from the environment) which made sexual reproduction possible (and which exponentially increased the level of genetic diversity on the planet by guaranteeing that each offspring of sexually reproducing species of organisms would not be a mere genetic clone of some parent, but instead, each new organism produced via sexual reproduction would be a genetic hybrid which inherits 50% of its genome from one parent and 50% of its genome from the other parent).

The individual members of a sexually reproducing species are either male or else female. A male produces sperm cells (i.e. male gametes) and female produces egg cells (i.e. female gametes).

A zygote is an egg cell which has been fertilized by a sperm cell such that the zygote can then divide into two cells (and those cells divide into two cells, and so on, until the multicellular result formulates all the distinct body parts it needs to become a member of its species).

A gamete (otherwise known as a haploid) is a cell which only contains half of the genes it would otherwise have if it were a diploid (and a zygote is a diploid since it has the full set of chromosomes for building a whole organism whose genome has the same number of genes as the body which produced its constituent gametes).

asexual_reproduction : clone :: sexual_reproduction : hybrid.

Hermaphroditic species such as banana slugs exist today which are capable of sexually reproducing with any adult member of its species. Hermaphrodites are organisms which exhibit a complete set of reproductive organs and the capacity to use those organs to produce offspring. Banana slugs likely acquired such a feature because it allowed them to reproduce faster than they would if their species was sexually dimorphic (and banana slugs tend to be spaced far apart from each other and travel slowly such that they rarely encounter other sexually mature banana slugs before the end of their individual life spans).

Human individuals have historically been sexually differentiated as either being male or else female. Furthermore, the sexual dimorphism and cultural notions of masculinity (i.e. arbitrarily male-associated qualities) and femininity (i.e. arbitrarily female-associated qualities) in human civilization has been quite elaborate and subjectively important to most humans.
