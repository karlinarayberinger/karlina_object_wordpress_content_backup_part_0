CONSCIOUSNESS

SOLIPSISM: the sensory perceptions and logical conceptualizations which suggest that exactly one locus of consciousness exists.

PANPSYCHISM: the epistemological framework which conceptualizes all phenomena (i.e. qualia) as being suspended inside of exactly one substrate made of consciousness (i.e. nothingness).

QUALIA: an object (or group of objects) consisting of distinct and static features for some finite interval of space and time.

NOTHINGNESS: a ubiquitous and featureless backdrop to all qualia (and permeating “substance” inside of all qualia).

CONSCIOUSNESS: the substrate which enables this sequence of characters to be instantiated.
